# Research #001 — Founding Report

## Which Public Crypto Trading Strategies Survive Independent Validation?

**Alpha Factory Research · July 2026**

---

## 1. Introduction

Between June and July 2026, Alpha Factory Research independently tested 57 public crypto trading strategies through a structured validation pipeline. This report covers the five strategies that reached the most advanced stages of testing — and why only one survived.

We do not provide trading signals. We publish evidence.

---

## 2. The Validation Pipeline

Every strategy passes through the same gates:

1. **Mechanism Discovery:** identify structural market edges.
2. **Data Feasibility Gate:** verify obtainable, clean historical data before any engineering.
3. **Reality Check:** test causal chain with frozen, preregistered backtests. No parameter search.
4. **Frozen Backtest:** preregistered event definitions, fixed costs, rejection criteria set before viewing results.
5. **Forward Evidence:** prospective paper trading before any capital. No backtest-alone claims.
6. **Cost & Concentration Audit:** fees, slippage, funding, and single-trade dominance analysis.

---

## 3. Strategy 1 — Liquidation Cascade Continuation (H037)

**Mechanism:** follow the liquidation cascade. When forced liquidations create price pressure, the cascade continues in the same direction.

**What happened:**
- 21 genuine forward trades collected over 12 days.
- Gross profit factor: 1.67. Gross expectancy: +1.14%.
- Passed all headline gates.

**The kill:**
Two PEPE trades contributed 257% of total net profit. Remove PEPE — the remaining 19 trades had a net profit factor of **0.30** and net expectancy of **-0.78%**. Bootstrap 95% CI contained zero.

**Lesson:** aggregate profitability can hide fatal concentration. Excluding the best symbol must not destroy the edge.

---

## 4. Strategy 2 — Stop-Hunt Reversal (H011)

**Mechanism:** liquidity grabs at obvious stop levels create a reversal opportunity.

**What happened:**
- 10 paper trades. Win rate 60%. Profit factor 1.72.
- One trade from the paper promotion gate.

**The block:**
Bootstrap confidence interval still contains zero. Not statistically significant. Strategy remains in evidence collection.

**Lesson:** positive expectancy at small N is not the same as statistical significance.

---

## 5. Strategy 3 — Cross-Venue Funding Dispersion (CVFD)

**Mechanism:** OKX-Binance funding rate spread reveals venue-specific positioning. The crowded venue is squeeze-vulnerable.

**What happened:**
- Killed in 45 minutes of independent frozen falsification.
- Zero cost. Free data.

**The kill:**
The spread is real (~0.1 bp) and maps to genuine positioning differences — but carries zero detectable directional information about future price. 0/6 price impact tests significant. 0/12 bootstrap CIs exclude zero.

**Lesson:** a signal can be real, structural, and still have no tradeable edge.

---

## 6. Strategy 4 — Volatility Compression (虎爪)

**Mechanism:** BB bandwidth compression signals an impending breakout.

**What happened:**
- 3 days of live trading. 93 filled buy orders. Real capital deployed.
- Gross PnL: +$53.61. Net PnL: **-$46.22**.
- 177 operations in 5 days. Costs consumed the edge.

**The kill:**
Configuration drift from V1 to V2 (removing BTC filter, shortening holding period) turned a marginally positive strategy into a clearly negative one. Cross-strategy interference from V6 forced premature exits.

**Lesson:** high turnover kills edge through fees. Mid-live configuration changes contaminate evidence.

---

## 7. Strategy 5 — Pre-Market Convergence (DFG-009)

**Mechanism:** OKX pre-market perpetual prices converge to spot as the conversion deadline approaches.

**What happened:**
- 16 terminal events identified. Census reconstructed from official announcements.
- 10 executable paired trades.

**The kill:**
The premium widened instead of contracting during the conversion window. Net profit factor: **0.018**. The market correctly priced the novelty premium — there was no delayed absorption to exploit.

**Lesson:** a successful product lifecycle is not automatically a profitable trading strategy.

---

## 8. What Survived

One strategy passed all gates: **M7 Unlock Events**. 63.4% directional accuracy out-of-sample. But it remains in evidence collection — event frequency is low and statistical significance requires more data.

The broader finding: **forced-flow mechanisms** (liquidation cascades, dealer hedging, institutional rebalancing) consistently outperform **discretionary-prediction mechanisms** (momentum, breakout, ML-based). When someone *must* act, the edge is structural. When someone *chooses* to act, the edge is statistical — and usually disappears.

---

## 9. Lessons for Systematic Traders

1. **Gross profit factor is not enough.** H037 had PF 1.67 — and would have lost money in production.
2. **Concentration kills.** Two trades out of 21 = 257% of total net profit. Ex-best-symbol is a mandatory robustness check.
3. **Forward evidence is mandatory.** No strategy should deploy capital based on backtest alone.
4. **Fees are not a detail.** 177 operations in 5 days destroyed a positive gross edge.
5. **Kill fast.** CVFD was falsified in 45 minutes for $0. The most expensive mistake is testing too long.
